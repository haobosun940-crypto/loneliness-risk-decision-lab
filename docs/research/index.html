About this study

A research-oriented behavioral assessment, not a clinical diagnosis.

The project measures late adolescents and emerging adults in digital peer contexts. It combines self-report loneliness and connection items with short decision tasks, then converts the responses into standardized indicators for scoring, visualization, and regression analysis.

Research question Does loneliness change how people choose risk?

Primary focus: loneliness and social connection as predictors of immediate rewards, impulsive spending, high-risk choices, and conflict-defense responses.

Measurement Survey items + behavioral choice tasks

The test uses Likert batteries, social-signal inputs, delay choices, spending scenarios, risky options, and conflict-response sliders.

Boundary Educational research tool only

Scores estimate a decision-profile index for research and class presentation. They are not mental-health diagnosis or clinical advice.

FAQ

Questions people usually ask before taking the test.

Is this a psychological diagnosis?+

No. The website is an educational research instrument. It estimates a decision-profile index and should not be used as clinical or mental-health diagnosis.

How long does the test take?+

Most participants can finish in about three minutes. The survey combines short social-signal questions, loneliness and connection items, and brief decision scenarios.

Is my response anonymous?+

The form does not ask for a name. Live submissions are stored on this server for research demonstration and CSV export, while synthetic pilot data remain clearly separated.

Can I retake the test?+

Yes. Retaking creates a new score. Use “Score without saving” if you want to see a profile without adding another live submission to the local database.

What population is the study designed for?+

The primary target group is late adolescents and emerging adults, especially students and young adults in digitally mediated school or college communities.

Why does the study measure social media behavior?+

Platform opens, messages, in-person contact, and trusted contacts are quantitative indicators of social exposure and connection. They help connect psychology constructs to observable behavior.

What does the Risk Decision Index mean?+

It is a 0-100 composite of immediate-reward preference, impulsive spending, high-risk choices, and conflict-defense responses. Higher values indicate a stronger short-term or risk-oriented decision pattern.

Are synthetic and live data mixed together?+

No. Synthetic pilot rows demonstrate the research pipeline. Live submissions are stored separately so the real sample can be analyzed without confusing it with simulated data.

Can this be used for a school research presentation?+

Yes. The site, PDF, workbook, and slides are designed to explain the full chain: research question, measurement, data collection, regression, ANOVA, visualization, and interpretation.
